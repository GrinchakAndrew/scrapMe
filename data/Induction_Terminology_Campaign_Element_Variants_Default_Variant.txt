<div style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border: 1px solid #6690BC; width: 98%; text-align: center;"><span style="text-align: center; line-height: 30px; font-variant: small-caps; color: #6690bc; font-size: 17px;">Elements: Maxyboxes, Examples</span>

                    <ul style="list-style-type: square;">
                        <li style="margin-bottom: 10px;"><span style="color: #000000;"><span>
Elements are the identifiable areas (both physical and logical) of a given website targeted by alternative content \ behavior for testing, sometimes referred to as a 'Maxybox', although the latter is obsolete and no longer in use officially. Examples of an Element include a landing page image, banners, or a copy of a Call-to-Action button that is to be tested. By logical website area we understand behavior of the tested website area. An Element may be a logical unit rather than a physical website area: in this case, Element would be responsible for changing behavior of a website area rather than replacing its assets.
