                        <p>
                            <span style="line-height: 1.5em; color: #000000; font-size: 10pt;">
 A/B testing (or MVT) is a means of learning about visitors' online behavior. Different visitors may fall within correlated segments, according to established patterns of consistent behavior. Segments, therefore, may be a subject to changes and adjustments, as testing is under way.
 </span></p>
 <p><span style="line-height: 1.5em; color: #000000; font-size: 10pt;">Visitor segments are distinct in their preferences. Serving personalized content for each segment will enhance conversion rates. Segmentation of users that are associated with different variants of content is accomplished by pre-assigned personalization rules, which are to be individually set up in the system (UI).</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5em; color: #000000; font-size: 10pt;">Whether grouping of visitors is conducted via Personalization Criteria towards segments formation, or via randomized generation of content (according to the established 'Variant Weightings'), the grouping remains valid throughout a predefined period of time (i.e., visitor is identifiable for corresponding content that has been generated). This period of time is tracked against expiry date that is stored in the 'pd' cookie. 'pd' cookie is also known as the 'persistent data' cookie.</span></p> 
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5em; color: #000000; font-size: 10pt;">This approach is part of Maxymiser solution, ensuring statistical consistency with one and the same visitor being shown the same content variation.</span></p>
